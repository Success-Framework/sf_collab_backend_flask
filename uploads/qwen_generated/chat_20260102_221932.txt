## Quantum Computing – A Friendly Overview

| **Concept** | **Classical (Traditional) Computer** | **Quantum Computer** |
|-------------|--------------------------------------|----------------------|
| **Basic unit** | **Bit** – 0 or 1 | **Qubit** – can be 0 *and* 1 *simultaneously* (superposition) |
| **How data is stored** | Two possible states (binary) | A “cloud” of possibilities – a probability cloud of 0 and 1 |
| **How data is processed** | Sequential logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, etc.) | Quantum gates that manipulate probability amplitudes, creating interference patterns |
| **Speed for certain tasks** | Exponential in some cases (e.g., brute‑force search) | Can solve some problems *exponentially faster* (e.g., factoring, simulation of molecules) |
| **Error handling** | Simple error correction (bit flips) | Requires sophisticated error‑correcting codes because qubits are fragile |

---

### 1. Bits vs. Qubits

- **Bits**: The “1” and “0” that make up every piece of data on a laptop or phone.  
- **Qubits**: Think of them like a spinning coin that, while spinning, is *both* heads **and** tails. They exist in a *superposition* of states until we measure them.

---

### 2. Superposition – “All at Once”

- A qubit can be in many states at once, described mathematically by a *wavefunction*.  
- When you run a computation, you’re effectively exploring all those states *simultaneously*.  
- After you measure the qubit, the wavefunction “collapses” to one definite state (0 or 1).
