# Quantum Computing in Plain English

Quantum computing is a new way of processing information that uses the strange rules of quantum physics instead of the everyday rules of classical computers.  
Think of it as a *super‑powered* calculator that can try many possibilities at once, rather than checking them one by one.

---

## 1. The Building Block: **Qubit** (Quantum Bit)

| Classical bit | Quantum bit (qubit) |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Can be only **0** or **1** | Can be **0**, **1**, or **both at the same time** (superposition) |

- **Superposition**: A qubit behaves like a coin that is spinning in the air. While the coin is spinning, it is *both* heads **and** tails simultaneously.
- When you *measure* a qubit, the spinning stops and you see either 0 or 1 – just like a coin lands on heads or tails.

---

## 2. Two Quantum Tricks That Matter

### a. Entanglement
- When two qubits become entangled, the state of one instantly tells you about the other, no matter how far apart they are.
- It’s like having two coins that always show opposite faces, even if one is on Earth and the other is on Mars.

### b. Interference
- By carefully adjusting qubits, we can make the “wrong” possibilities cancel each other out, while the “right” one is amplified.
- Imagine waves in a pond: some waves add together (constructive interference), others cancel out (destructive interference).

---

## 3. How a Quantum Computer Works

1. **Prepare Qubits**  
   Initialize many qubits in a known state (usually all 0).

2. **Apply Quantum Gates**  
   These are the quantum equivalent of logic gates (AND, OR, NOT).  
   Gates manipulate superpositions and entanglements, creating a *cloud* of possible solutions.

3. **Measure**  
   Collapse the qubits
